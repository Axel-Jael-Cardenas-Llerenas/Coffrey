Green te

Ingredients:
Sachets, leaves or pearls of green tea (1 teaspoon per cup of water approximately)
Hot water
5-6 leaves of Tulasi (plant also known as holy basil or Indian basil)
Honey
Lemon juice

Preparation:

Decide how many cups of green tea you want to prepare. In general, it is recommended to use one teaspoon (5 g) of green tea leaves or pearls per cup of water. A cup of water will be enough to prepare a cup of tea.
Measure the desired amount of green tea leaves or pearls and pour it into the strainer or filter.
Fill a pot of non-reactive material (such as glass or stainless steel) with water and heat it to about 80ºC (180ºF). You can use a kitchen thermometer to check the temperature, but if you do not have it, simply monitor the water and do not allow it to boil. Place the strainer or filter full of tea in an empty cup.
Pour the hot water into the cup, over the tea leaves.
Leave the leaves in infusion for 2-3 minutes. If you leave them for longer the tea will acquire a slightly bitter taste.
Take the strainer or filter out of the cup.
Let the infusion cool down a bit and enjoy your perfect cup of green tea.
Ready.
