Little horns

Ingredients:

1/3 cup of water
1 package of baking yeast
1 spoon of sugar
1 tablespoon of salt
1 1/3 cups of milk at a time

3 1/2 cups flour
2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
1 piece of egg
2 tablespoons of cream



Preparation:

In a small bowl, mix the water, the yeast and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Let stand for 5 minutes and make sure the yeast is reacting.
In another small deep bowl, dissolve the rest of the sugar and salt in the milk.
In a bowl the flour, the milk, the water with yeast and the oil have been added and the mixture with a wooden spatula.
On a surface with flour put the dough and the battery with the hands changing side each time for 2-3 minutes.
Transfer the dough to a container, cover with plastic wrap and let it grow for about an hour and a half. The dough should double in size.
Re-knead the dough on a surface with flour for 2-3 minutes and put in a refractory and cover with plastic wrap. Scroll up to 45 minutes in the refrigerator for the entire night.
On a surface with flour click on a butter base with a wooden spatula. This base should be 3/4 the size of the flattened dough.
On another surface is the dough with a baking roll. Put the butter on the dough and knead it to incorporate it 100%. Make a dough ball and cover with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for 1 hour.
Remove the dough from the refrigerator and the roller in a way of approximately 50 by 24 cms. If the butter is hard while it is melting.

Cut the rectangle in half and in each half make triangles, each triangle will be a horn.
Preheat the oven to 220 degrees Celsius and roll each triangle towards the tip
Put the little horns on baking trays with waxed paper underneath them and curl the edges of each little horn towards the center.
In a bowl whisk the egg yolk and the cream and with a brush paint the horns so that they take a golden color.
Cook the horns for 15 minutes or until golden brown. Remove from the oven.
